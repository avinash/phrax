What are Fractals?
 

A fractal is a geometric object which is rough or irregular on all scales of length, and so which appears to be 'broken up' in a radical way. Some of the best examples can be divided into parts, each of which is similar to the original object. Fractals are said to possess infinite detail, and they may actually have a self-similar structure that occurs at different levels of magnification. In many cases, a fractal can be generated by a repeating pattern, in a typically recursive or iterative process. In other words, a fractal object has two basic characteristics:

  • Infinite detail at every point,
  • A certain self-similarity between object parts and the overall features of the objects.
