Fractals in Nature
 

Approximate fractals are easily found in nature. These objects display complex structure over an extended, but finite, scale range. Examples of naturally occurring fractals in nature are:

  • Clouds
  • Mountains
  • River networks
  • System of blood vessels
  • Trees and ferns

These naturally occurring fractals can be modelled on a computer using a recursive algorithm. For instance, in the case of trees, a branch from a tree is a miniature replica of the whole: not identical, but similar in nature.
